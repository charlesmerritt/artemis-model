Why this de-risks the scheduler. A county TPO cap is a global constraint: at each barrier the schedule looks across all stands, cuts the ones the allocation selects, and lets the rest grow. These checks show every part of that loop — pause, selectively cut, resume, repeat — matches a native FVS run to the last digit, on one stand and on a multi-stand bundle alike.
The one FAIL is carbon, and it's deliberately parked. Stop/restart silently understates one FFE carbon pool (~8% per barrier) while stand values stay bit-identical. Carbon is out of scope for v1 (carbon_extension: false, guarded by a tripwire test) and must be resolved before any carbon result is reported externally. Fixtures here are narrow — 6 stands, one variant, one FVS version, no fire — so clean results on untested pools remain provisional.
